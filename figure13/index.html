Motivation

Khmer textlines are typically 3–5× wider than equivalent Latin text due to complex stacked consonants and diacritics. Training the Transformer on full-width images causes O(n²) self-attention cost to explode. The chunking algorithm slices each textline into fixed-width overlapping windows, processes each independently, then merges predictions using confidence scores to reconstruct the full text.

Step 5 — Merge Predictions
5
Combine high-confidence predictions from each chunk's center region
Merging Rules
For each position in the final output, the algorithm selects the prediction from the chunk where that pixel position falls in the center region (not the overlap zone). Overlap regions are discarded in favour of the adjacent chunk's center prediction.
✅
Center region (px O … W-O)
Characters whose position falls in the inner 68 px zone are kept from this chunk's prediction.
🔄
Overlap margin (O = 16 px)
Characters in the left/right 16 px margins are deferred to the adjacent chunk which sees them in its center.
✂️
Duplicate predictions
Predictions in overlap zones may be duplicated across chunks — the merge step removes duplicates deterministically.

The output is a complete Khmer string reconstructed character-by-character. Because each chunk always sees only 100 px, the model's attention computation cost is bounded regardless of textline length, while the merging step ensures no characters are missed or duplicated at chunk boundaries.

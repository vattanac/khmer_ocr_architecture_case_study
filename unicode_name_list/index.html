Dependent Vowel Signs & Consonant Shifters U+17B6 – U+17CA

Two-part dependent vowel signs (U+17BE–U+17C0, U+17C4–U+17C5) have glyph pieces on both sides of the consonant. They follow the consonant in logical order and should be handled as a unit for processing.

Various Signs & Punctuation U+17C6 – U+17DD
Glyph Code Name Notes
◌ំU+17C6 Khmer Sign Nikahit = srak am / anusvara · Final nasalization · Usually regarded as vowel sign am
ះU+17C7 Khmer Sign Reahmuk = srak ah / visarga
ៈU+17C8 Khmer Sign Yuukaleapintu Inserts short inherent vowel with abrupt glottal stop; transliteration: yukaleakpintu
◌់U+17CB Khmer Sign Bantoc Shortens the vowel sound; transliteration: bantak
◌៌U+17CC Khmer Sign Robat Diacritic historically corresponding to repha form of ra in Devanagari
◌៍U+17CD Khmer Sign Toandakhiat Indicates the base character is not pronounced
◌៎U+17CE Khmer Sign Kakabat Used with some exclamations
◌៏U+17CF Khmer Sign Ahsda Denotes stressed intonation in some single-consonant words
◌័U+17D0 Khmer Sign Samyok Sannya Denotes deviation from pronunciation rules; mostly loan words from Pali/Sanskrit, French
◌៑U+17D1 Khmer Sign Viriam Mostly obsolete "killer"; final consonant without inherent vowel sound
◌្U+17D2 Khmer Sign Coeng Indicates following letter is rendered subscripted; shape is arbitrary and not visibly rendered
◌៓U+17D3 Khmer Sign Bathamasat Originally for lunar date symbols; use discouraged in favor of complete set (→ U+19E0)
។U+17D4 Khmer Sign Khan Functions as a full stop / period
៕U+17D5 Khmer Sign Bariyoosan Indicates end of a section or text
៖U+17D6 Khmer Sign Camnuc Pii Kuuh Functions as colon; transliteration: camnoc pii kuuh
ៗU+17D7 Khmer Sign Lek Too Repetition sign
៘U+17D8 Khmer Sign Beyyal Et cetera; use discouraged — preferred: U+17D4 U+179B U+17D4
៙U+17D9 Khmer Sign Phnaek Muan Indicates beginning of a book or treatise; transliteration: phnek moan
៚U+17DA Khmer Sign Koomuut Indicates end of a book; pairs with U+17D9 ៙; transliteration: koomoot
៛U+17DB Khmer Currency Symbol Riel Currency symbol for the Cambodian riel (KHR)
ៜU+17DC Khmer Sign Avakrahasanya Rare; shows omitted Sanskrit vowel, like an apostrophe; transliteration: avakraha sannya
◌៝U+17DD Khmer Sign Atthacan Mostly obsolete; final consonant with its inherent vowel sound
